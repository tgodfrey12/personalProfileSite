About Me

Born in the midwest and with an "early to bed, early to rise" midwest work ethic, I started in tech for a basement-based startup in 1994. I helped users of a medical patient records software configure printers and use their Windows 3.1 and Windows NT. Later I supported UNIX operating systems while working my way through college full time. After getting my Bachelors degree in computer science in early 2001, I was hired to develop software using Java 2. I migrated to the Microsoft development stack in 2007 and began to specialize in middleware and backend development. The latest shift began in 2017, attending the immersive web development course at Galvanize from which I graduated in July 2017.
